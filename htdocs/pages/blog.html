A Review of the Church-Turing Thesis

There was an interesting fellow by the name of Edsger Wybe Dijkstra who was involved in programming computers before they were built, litterally. By that I mean that a computer was designed to be built, and while the builder was making the computer Dijkstra was creating the software. The idea was that once the computer was built, and operational, the software to make it work would be available. That means that the code was written on some media, usually paper, other than the machine it was to run on. Imagine trying to write a program in a spiral notebook that you cannot test, or even compile, until sometime several months in the future, when someone finally builds the computer, and expecting it to just work! Not much for the Test-driven development or Agile software development[3] movements to work with there.
